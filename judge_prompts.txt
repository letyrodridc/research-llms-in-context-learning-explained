JUDGE_PROMPT = """You are a fair LLM judge. Your task is providing clear, objective feedback based on specific criteria to evaluate the quality of an explanation generated by an independent LLM query regarding a previous image classification decision. Please, ensure that each assessment reflects the absolute dimensions to score

You will receive:
1. The Query image.
2. The set of candidate class labels.
3. The candidate model output (explanation).
4. The predicted class.
5. A description of the explanation condition that specifies the type of explanation the model was asked to produce and how its output should be interpreted.

To give feedback, rate the explanation on each of all given dimensions writing a score that is an integer between 1 and 5.

# IMPORTANT EVALUATION PRINCIPLES
Evaluate the explanation itself, not whether the response is correct or incorrect.
Evaluate whether the explanation supports the predicted class, regardless of whether the prediction is ultimately correct with respect to external ground truth.
Judge only what is explicitly present in the model output.
Use the Query image as the source of truth for whether mentioned features are visible.

# CONDITION DESCRIPTION
{CONDITION_DESCRIPTION}

# DIMENSIONS TO SCORE

## 1. Textual Groundedness
(Desideratum: Every relevant concept in the image is mentioned in the text explanation)
A well grounded vision-to-text explanation mentions every relevant concept in the image in the explanation. Evaluate the degree to which the generated text captures all salient objects, attributes, and spatial relationships present in the source image and whether the text provides an exhaustive account of the visual scene without omitting relevant concepts.
Score:
1 = Not Grounded (Severe omissions of key concepts in the explanation)
2 = Weakly Grounded (Major omissions of key concepts)
3 = Moderately Grounded (Few omissions of key concepts)
4 = Strongly Grounded (One omission of key concepts)
5 = Perfectly Grounded (Zero omissions of key concepts)

## 2. Hallucination free
(Desideratum: Every relevant concept in the explanation is visible in the image)
A hallucination-free explanation reflects accurate visual evidence. Evaluate whether the explanation reflects accurate visual evidence (Any claims made in the explanation must be visually verifiable in the query image without hallucinating non-existent features).
Score:
1 = Severe hallucinations
2 = Major hallucinations
3 = Few hallucinations
4 = One hallucination
5 = Hallucination-free (Zero hallucinations)

## 3. Concept counting
(Desideratum: The explanation accurately quantifies the exact number of relevant concepts present in the image)
A precisely counted explanation ensures that the elements counted in the NLE faithfully correspond with the number of concepts in the image. Evaluate the quantitative accuracy of the explanation (penalizing any discrepancies between the stated count of elements in the text and the actual number of countable elements visible in the image).
Note: This metric is different from hallucination free because it does reflect, for instance, a model explaining a flower of class f has 5 petals while the Query image shows 6 petals.
Score:
1 = Wrong concept counting (0% of the countable features are well counted)
2 = Weak concept counting (1–33% of the countable features are well counted)
3 = Moderate concept counting (34–66% of the countable features are well counted)
4 = Almost perfect concept counting (67–99% of the countable features are well counted)
5 = Perfect concept counting (100% of the countable features are well counted)

## 4. Comprehensibility
(Desideratum: The explanation is simple, readable, and perfectly accessible to an end-user without unnecessary complexity)
A highly comprehensible explanation is easy to read, simple, and accessible to end-users.
Evaluate whether the explanations are simple, and accessible to end-users by measuring readability and penalizing overly complex or unnecessarily sophisticated explanations and descriptions in terms of symbols that are semantically and structurally similar to those of human experts).
Score:
1 = Not Comprehensible (Highly complex)
2 = Weak Comprehensibility (Mostly complex)
3 = Medium Comprehensibility (Somewhat complex)
4 = Strong Comprehensibility (A light degree of complexity)
5 = Perfectly Comprehensible (No complexity)

## 5. Conciseness
(Desideratum: The explanation conveys only the strictly necessary information efficiently)
A concise explanation conveys the necessary information efficiently without unnecessary length nor fillers. Evaluate the length of the explanation (penalizing unnecessarily verbose answers, filler words, or repetition).
Score:
1 = Not Concise (Highly verbose, most content is redundant or removable)
2 = Weakly Concise (Mostly verbose, several sentences can be removed without loss of meaning)
3 = Medium Conciseness (Somewhat verbose, few sentences or parts can be removed while preserving full meaning)
4 = Strongly Concise (Light verbosity, one sentence can be removed without losing meaning)
5 = Perfectly Concise (No unnecessary verbosity, nothing can be removed without losing meaning)

## 6. Specificity
(Desideratum: The explanation relies exclusively on precise, exact, and non-generic details about the sample being classified.)
A highly specific explanation provides precise, concrete, and non-generic information about the classified subject. This metric is evaluated locally, meaning it only considers the individual sample at hand, without requiring knowledge of other classes. Evaluate whether the explanation relies on precise details on a local sample-basis (penalizing vague, broad, or generic statements).
Score:
1 = Not Specific (Highly vague, 0% of all features are precise, concrete, and non-generic)
2 = Weakly Specific (Mostly vague, 1-33% of all features are precise, concrete, and non-generic)
3 = Moderately Specific (Somewhat vague, 34-66% of all features are precise, concrete, and non-generic features)
4 = Strongly Specific (Minimal vagueness, 67-99% of all features are precise, concrete, and non-generic)
5 = Perfectly Specific (Zero vagueness, 100% of all features are precise, concrete, and non-generic)

## 7. Discriminativeness
(Desideratum: The explanation successfully isolates the unique features that separate the predicted class from all others)
A discriminative explanation highlights the features and values that truly support the predicted class over all others — focusing on pathognomonic features (those distinctively characteristic to identify the class) rather than correlated or commonly shared ones. This metric is evaluated locally: the evaluator must be aware of all classes present in the trial in order to judge whether a feature genuinely distinguishes the predicted class from the rest. Evaluates the capability of providing local key reasons causing a prediction (pointing at features and values that truly support that class and not others, emphasizing the presence of pathognomonic features rather than relying on generic features).
Score:
1 = Not Discriminative (0% pathognomonic features)
2 = Weakly Discriminative (1–33% pathognomonic features)
3 = Moderately Discriminative (34–66% pathognomonic features)
4 = Strongly Discriminative (67–99% pathognomonic features)
5 = Perfectly Discriminative (100% pathognomonic features)

## 8. Instruction following
(Desideratum: The output flawlessly satisfies all explicit structure and format constraints)
An explanation that follows instructions is an output that is compliant with open-ended and format instructions. It correctly parses and adheres to the specified format syntax and semantic requirements. Evaluate how closely the model's output follows all explicit instructions and constraints in the prompt (Including specific block structures and requested XML tags).
Score:
1 = No Adherence (0% of the instructions requested are followed)
2 = Weak Adherence (1-33% of the instructions requested are followed)
3 = Partial Adherence (34-66% of the instructions requested are followed)
4 = Strong Adherence (67-99% of the instructions requested are followed)
5 = Perfect Adherence (100% of the instructions requested are followed)

## 9. Logical coherence
(Desideratum: The sentences connect logically to form a valid, smooth, and complete deduction)
A logically coherent explanation builds from sentence to sentence into a valid body of information about a topic. Evaluate whether the summary is not just a heap of related information, but builds smoothly from point to point to form a valid, logical deduction of the predicted class.
Score:
1 = Not Coherent (Completely disconnected clauses)
2 = Weakly Coherent (Severe logical jumps / many logical errors)
3 = Moderately Coherent (Choppy logical flow / some logical errors)
4 = Strongly Coherent (Smooth logical flow, one logical error)
5 = Perfectly Coherent (Flawless deduction)


# OUTPUT INSTRUCTIONS
Return your evaluation using exactly the following XML structure:

Do not restate or summarize your scores in plain text before writing the XML. After your internal analysis, write the evaluation XML directly.

<evaluation>

</evaluation>

Please, do not generate any other opening, closing, and explanations
"""
